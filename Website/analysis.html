This bar graph shows the relationship each article group has with each other. We were trying to see which gender got quoted more often with the news articles and we think we came up with a valid conclusion. Males have been quoted more often, 5 out of the 6 article groups we looked at, than women, 1 out of the 6 article groups. The only article group that had women out quoted the men was the Women's March articles which is no surprise there. There were a total of 238 total quotes that were used throughout all the articles that we looked at. 168 of those were said by men, 47 were said by female, and 23 were said by groups of people. 71% of the articles were made up of quotes developed by men while only about 20% of the articles were made up of female quotes. There were a total of 29 quotes inside the election results articles; 28 were male and 1 was female. In the syrian bombing articles, there was a total of 93 quotes; 74 being male, 2 being female, and 17 being groups of people. In the articles about the travel ban, there was a total of 47 quotes; 40 being male, 6 being female, and 1 being a group of people. In the articles about Trump's taxes, there were only 19 total quotes used; 14 being male, 0 were female, and 5 were by groups of people. The last group of articles we looked at were about the women's march. There were a total of 50 quotes used; 12 being men and 38 being women.

This bar graph shows the relationship each news source has with each other in terms of using gender in quotes. We were trying to see which gender got quoted more often with the news stations and we think we came up with a valid conclusion. Males have been quoted more often overall between all four news sources. BBC had a total of 58 quotes in the five articles we looked at. 31 of them were male and 11 of them were female and the rest, 16, were a group of people. CNN had a total of 77 quotes in the articles with 59 of them being male, 13 of them being female, and 5 of them being groups of people. Next we have FOX. FOX had a total of 57 quotes (very similar to BBC); 45 of them being male, 11 of them being female, and only 1 being a group of people. The final station we covered was NPR. They had 46 total quotes in their selected articles. 33 of those quotes were given by men, 12 were given by female, and 11 were given by groups of people.

In this network, the green lines signal a BBC article, the blue lines signal a CNN article, the red lines signal a FOX article and the yellow lines signal an NPR article. The largest circles contain the different topics of the articles and the lines connect the emotional language words used in the articles based on the their topic. The words inside larger circles are used the most throughout the articles and the edge lines connect the words used for more than one topic by the source of the article that uses it. The closer the word is to the center of the network the more variety there is in the topics that use them. The larger the word circle is, the more times it is used overall.
